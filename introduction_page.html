Get to know me!

    My name is Ana Bindiu! I am a third year computer science student at University of Calgary. I was born in Romania in a small city called
Arad. Growing up, I learned to love to think analytically as I was fascinated by the challenges I took in my math classes. My family is full of artists,
my parents being interior designers, with my sister following in their footsteps. Despite that, I always knew my career in life would be different
than that. I've always loved analyzing everything around me, from detecting patterns in my life to interpreting information around me to taking
decisions about which action to take next. My fascination with technology started when I was young, as I became curious to know how things
in a computer work. Unfortunately, as I grew older, despite my constant love for analytical thinking and technology, I felt lonely as a female in
that kind of field. Due to societal standards, I then prioritized more feminine likes such as biology and chemistry and hoped for a different future.
Moving to Canada in 2014, I worked towards my goal at the time of becoming a doctor. As I finished high school, I started college at Mount Royal
University, studying chemistry. After my first semester, I had a strong gut feeling that what I was studying was not meant to be for me. Talking to
multiple people in my life, a lot of people suggested I try a few computer science courses. In my second semester at Mount Royal University, I took
a full course load of computer science courses with the hope I'd fit in. Despite the fact that I was one of the 4 girls in my classes, I knew this was
something I wanted to do. A few months later I got accepted at University of Calgary and decided to follow this path. Though computer science
courses were very challenging to me in my first year of college, I am proud to say I am in my third year of computer science now feeling more
achieved than ever. I am very proud of the progress I've done and the amount of things I've learned.
    I am very proud of the computer scientists out there and the solutions they've come up with for diverse technological problems and the
amount of technological growth there has been due to the relentless problem solving of computer scientists out there. I hope to be one of those
computer scientists who helps solve problems around the world and I want to gain all the knowledge needed to do that! I also want to be an
encourager to women out there who have a passion for this type of field and feel as though they don't fit in!
